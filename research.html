Research

My research in astrophysics during college

Evolution of the Star-Forming Main Sequence (SFMS)

In my undergraduate thesis, I studied the SFMS across cosmic time. I used data from the JWST and HST in four observational fields. I performed the SED fitting procedure to extract physical properties of over 45 thousands star-forming galaxies. I found that SFMS normalization increases with redshift, while the slope has a valley at redshift z ~ 4 - 5 .

Spatially Resolved Scale of the SFMS

In my graduate thesis, I continued to study physical processes that driven the SFMS evolution. I took a sample of 15% star-forming galaxies from my undergraduate work. To study the physical processes, I perfomed the spatially resolved SED fitting. Then, I analyze the galactic radial profiles at 2 < z < 8 . It found that the SFMS evolution may be driven by the inside-out growth theory.
